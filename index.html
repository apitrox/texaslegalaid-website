How It Works

Getting free legal help is simple. Our streamlined process connects you with the right legal aid provider in just a few steps.

1. Tell Us Your Situation

Answer a few simple questions about your legal issue. We'll keep your information confidential and secure.

2. Get Matched

We connect you with legal aid providers who specialize in your type of case and serve your area.

3. Receive Legal Help

A legal professional will review your case and provide guidance, representation, or resources to help you.

Yes — Texas Legal Aid is free for the people who use it. Legal Aid is a type of welfare program, like other welfare programs for food, housing, child care, and healthcare, you must first qualify and once qualified, you receive the benefits of the program at no cost to you. Texas Legal Aid, like other welfare programs, does cost money to operate, but it is funded largely through government grants of public funds. Legal Aid Providers are essentially paid by state public funds to provide legal assistance to eligible individuals at no cost to them.

Eligibility typically depends largely on your income and the type of services needed. The vast majority of legal aid services in Texas require a person to be at or below 125% of the federal poverty level. Some programs and people allow for higher income limits, for example military veterans and survivors of sexual assault may qualify at higher income levels. Always contact us to find out what programs you may qualify for.

To determine your poverty level yourself, you can use the federal poverty guidelines available on the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services website. Poverty is determined on a household size and income basis for all members of a household, not on an individual basis.

After you submit your request, you'll typically hear from a legal aid provider within 1-3 business days. For urgent matters like imminent eviction or domestic violence, we prioritize your case for faster response. You can track your case status in "My Cases" at any time.
