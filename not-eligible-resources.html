Harris County Law Library Self Help Center

The self-help center can assist pro se litigants in filling out forms and if necessary, providing appropriate referrals. This service is primarily for family law cases. Click here for more information on services and changes. Those seeking assistance should call (713) 228-0734. You will then be directed to a voicemail. An HVL staff member will then return your phone call and obtain the information needed to set up your meeting with an attorney. Regular hours are Monday – Friday 8:30 a.m. - 5 p.m. First come, first serve.

Travis County Law Library Self-help Center

For those representing themselves in Travis County civil courts, the Law Library's Self-Help Center can provide forms and information. In many agreed cases, the reference attorneys can meet with family law litigants to answer questions and review paperwork. If you have an uncontested family law case, in Travis County, you must have your forms reviewed by a reference attorney at the Law Library's Walk-In Case Review before going to court to finish your case.

Please click here for more information on Walk-In Case Review and Reference Attorney Services.

Harris County Dispute Resolution Center

The Dispute Resolution Center (DRC) is sponsored by the Houston Bar Association and funded through the Harris County Courts System. The DRC has been helping people resolve disputes since 1980. Services are FREE to residents of Harris County, Texas. Hours of Operation: 8:00 AM - 5:00 PM Monday - Friday. (713) 274-7100

Services Provided:

  • General Disputes (no lawsuit yet) — This program is for the mediation of disputes before a lawsuit is filed (except for divorce).
  • Divorce (both with and without a lawsuit) — This program is for the mediation of divorce disputes both before and after a lawsuit is filed. Common disputes in this program involve: Divorce - child: custody, visitation, support, etc.; Modification - child support, visitation, etc.; Grandparent Access / Custody; Property Division - pre & post-divorce; LGBT - all issues associated with divorce; Common-Law separation.

2-1-1 Texas — This program offers accurate, well-organized, and easy-to-find information, local health and human services programs, and other local programs you may find helpful. 2-1-1 Texas is a free, anonymous social service hotline available 24 hours a day, seven days a week, 365 days a year.

No matter where you live in Texas, you can dial 2-1-1 or (877) 541-7905, and find information about resources in your local community. Whether you need help finding food or housing, childcare, crisis counseling, or substance abuse treatment, one number is all you need to know.

Boat People SOS Houston Legal Services Center

BPSOS-Houston's Legal Services Center is a non-profit law firm serving the legal needs of low-income individuals in the Vietnamese and wider Asian communities of Houston. Due to COVID precautions, legal consultations are by phone only. Apply over the telephone by calling (281) 530-6888.

Their mission is to provide meaningful access to high-quality, civil legal services that are culturally sensitive and linguistically appropriate to low-income persons and members of vulnerable populations throughout Harris and surrounding counties. They provide reduced fees and free services to those who qualify.

Dallas Volunteer Attorney Program

The Dallas Volunteer Attorney Program provides legal help to low-income clients with the following types of cases: Benefits, Consumer, Employment, Family, Housing, Landlord-Tenant, Bankruptcy, Expunctions & Non-disclosures, Probate, Real Property, Tax, Wills. Call (888) 548-8457 for intake and eligibility for legal advice.

Houston Volunteer Lawyers

HVL provides free legal help to eligible low-income residents of the Greater Houston area. They specialize in family, housing, wills, veterans' issues, taxes, and other non-criminal matters. To find out if Houston Volunteer Lawyers can help you, please tell them about yourself and your legal concern by taking the questionnaire.

Volunteer Legal Services of Central Texas

VLS has suspended in-person legal intake and advice clinics until they can safely hold in-person clinics again. However, individuals can receive no-cost legal advice over the phone through licensed attorney volunteers. To apply for legal assistance with Volunteer Legal Services (VLS), you must apply online and participate in our legal advice and intake phone clinic. Our application period opens at 9:00 a.m. on Mondays and ends at 5:00 p.m. on Wednesdays or earlier if capacity is reached. Instructions on how to apply online will be posted here.

NOTE: VLS does not handle immigration or pending criminal law matters. VLS may close applications early if requests for legal services exceed VLS capacity to provide services.

FREE LAW STUDENT REPRESENTATION

Many law schools host programs that allow law students to practice law under the supervision and guidance of licensed attorneys. Often times they only take a limited amount of cases each semester and limit the clients they take to persons of limited financial means. Below is a list of Texas law schools that host legal clinic programs that may be able to help you with your family law issue.

Randall O. Sorrels Legal Clinic at South Texas College of Law

Law students at South Texas College of Law represent low-income (125%-200% of the federal poverty guideline) clients under the supervision and guidance of licensed attorneys. If you would like to apply for Legal Assistance, please APPLY HERE or call (713) 646-2990. Among other services not listed here, their services include:

  • Family Law - Basic — Divorces with minimal property and no children
  • Family Law - Advanced — Students represent clients in cases that present more advanced, mixed issues than found in a simple divorce with no children or any substantial property. Here client issues tend to focus on divorce joined with contested property, children and custody issues, parentage, grandparent access, post-judgment enforcement or modification, and state agency involvement. The Clinic also serves in court-appointed positions including amicus and ad litem appointments.

SMU VanSickle Family Law Clinic

Law student associates represent and provide counsel to low-income clients in matters of family law such as divorce, child custody, visitation, paternity, modifications, enforcement actions, child support, and adoption.

For questions, please call (214) 768-2562 or email them VanSickleFamilyLaw@smu.edu.

St. Mary's Civil Justice Family Law Clinic

Call (210) 431-2596 to find out more about these clinics and whether they might be able to provide help in your case. Please note that the clinics cannot handle cases requiring immediate attention. Student attorneys represent clients in state court with divorces, child custody, visitation, adoptions, and name changes.

Texas A&M Family University School of Law

Call (817) 212-4123 — Legal matters they can help with include: protective orders, family violence issues, physical or emotional abuse, divorces, child custody, child support, terminations of paternity and adoptions, other family law or child-related issues.

Texas Southern University Family Law Clinic

Call (713) 313-7275 — Only accepts cases in Harris County, Texas; cases are limited to the following: uncontested divorces with no children or property; uncontested divorces with children, minimum property. Clients are screened for eligibility based on the Federal Poverty Guidelines. However, the answers you provided on this form show that you are likely under 125% of the federal poverty guidelines, which makes it likely that you qualify.

University of Houston Civil Practice Clinic

Call (713) 743-2094 — Students in the Civil Practice Clinic work on a wide variety of civil cases, including bankruptcy, collection, landlord-tenant, divorce, domestic violence, administrative appeals, adoption, will drafting, guardianships, name changes, and probate. They only accept cases for a short period prior to the start of new semesters.

LEGAL LINES

Dallas Bar Association

Volunteer attorneys will answer legal questions at no cost from 4:00 p.m. to 8:00 p.m. on Wednesdays via a LegalLine clinic, sponsored by the Dallas Bar Association. To participate, complete the online form found here. A volunteer attorney will call the participant to provide up to 15 minutes of free legal information for your legal issue. Space is limited. Registration closes each Tuesday at noon.

Houston Bar Association

LegalLine is a community service outreach program created by the Houston Bar Association in 1985. This hotline is designed to help the people of Houston receive simple legal advice, answers to legal questions, and referrals to helpful resources over the telephone, at no cost to the caller. Callers may ask questions on any legal topic.

All calls are answered by licensed attorneys. Their volunteer attorneys specialize in a variety of fields. The volunteers remain anonymous.

No appointment is necessary; just place your call while the program is taking place. They accept phone calls only — not in-person visits. Please note that when the LegalLine program is not taking place, there are no attorneys who can be reached at this phone number. Please call (713) 759-1133. With few exceptions, LegalLine takes place on the first and third Wednesdays of every month, between 5 p.m. and 9 p.m.

Lawyer Referral Service of Central Texas

On the first Tuesday of every month, obtaining good, simple legal advice is easy!

Just call (512) 472-8303 for LegalLine, the free legal advice hotline. Phones are answered between 5:30 PM and 7:30 PM. Serving residents of Travis, Hays, Bastrop, and Williamson counties.

LegalLine is sponsored by Austin Lawyer Referral Service and is staffed by attorney volunteers who answer legal questions and give brief legal advice.

Calls are completely confidential and anonymous. Attorneys who are bilingual (English/Spanish) are also available.

Tarrant County Bar Association

LegalLine is a community service program offered twice a month by the Tarrant County Bar Association. Anyone interested should call LegalLine at (817) 335-1239.

Volunteer attorneys from the Tarrant County Bar Association offer free advice to Tarrant County residents on the 2nd and 4th Thursday of every month from 6 p.m. to 8 p.m.

As part of the Tarrant County Bar Association's commitment to the public, local lawyers volunteer two hours of their time to answer questions covering a broad range of topics. In addition to legal questions, individuals can ask for referrals to local legal or social service agencies.

LAWYER REFERRAL SERVICE

Texas Lawyer Referral Service

Texas Lawyer Referral Service provides free referrals. Their attorneys provide a free consultation. Their intake agents answer the phone 24/7 and are multilingual. After the initial consultation, the lawyers charge customary legal fees. To start your referral, text or call (512) 872-4400.
