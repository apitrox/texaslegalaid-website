Texas Family Help Link

Connecting children and caregivers in Texas with local legal resources.

Welcome!

Our goal is to connect children and caregivers in Texas with local legal resources.

Please apply ONLY if your legal issue involves a child's health, stability, safety, or security.

Filling out this online intake form does not create an attorney-client relationship, and it does NOT guarantee legal representation.

Self Help Resources

First, we'll ask some questions about who needs legal help.

In general, a child's parent or caregiver will apply on behalf of the child.

However, a child under the age of 17 may apply for themselves if they are in foster care, homeless, or they feel unsafe in their home. Help may also be available for young adults between the age of 18-25 who are transitioning out of foster care, or who have a mental or physical health condition or disability.

Family Helpline

Speak with an experienced child welfare attorney about Child Protective Services (CPS) issues in Texas. Our attorneys will make sure you know your rights and understand your legal options.

All calls are anonymous. To protect your identity, referrals will not be made through Family Help Link. Instead, call the Family Helpline directly at 844-888-5565.

Hours: Monday - Friday, 9:00 AM - 6:00 PM

The Family Helpline provides free legal information and can connect you with resources and referrals. However, the Family Helpline cannot represent you in court.

We also need to find out who the "opposing party" is. This means other people who will be involved in the lawsuit.

Need Immediate Legal Help?

If you need immediate legal assistance or have questions about your options, you can:

  • Call the Texas Legal Services Center at 1-800-622-2520
  • Visit TexasLawHelp.org for free legal information and resources
  • Contact your local county bar association for lawyer referral services
